Welcome to My MBA Blog!

Hi there! Thanks for stopping by. My name's Matt, and I'm a software engineer, among other things. I'm also a part-time student at Carnegie Mellon University's Tepper School of Business, working towards my Masters in Business Administration.

It's been a bit of a winding road to get to this point. I studied Anthropology and Music in college while holding down jobs working with A/V-related tech and equipment on the campus special events and recording crews. After graduation, I completed one year of service each with AmeriCorps NCCC and AmeriCorps VISTA, and fell in love with the program. Afterwards, following a period of figuring out how to best merge my civic-oriented passions with the troubleshooting and tinkering skills I learned through my jobs in undergrad, I came to the realization that learning how to code would enable me to have an even bigger impact on things. (I was particularly inspired by organizations like 412 Food Rescue and Civic Actions.) With that in mind, I took a leap and enrolled in the Tech Elevator coding bootcamp program in 2019, and have worked in technology ever since.

As of late summer 2023, I've been in the tech field for four years now, but it often still feels like I'm just starting out. That said, I've worked to develop robotic process automation workflows to assist internal IT teams at a large national bank, and built out customized chat platform integrations to further streamline how information is communicated across departments. Currently, I work on the IT side of things at Carnegie Mellon University. As a member of a small development team, I build and maintain a variety of applications that enable faculty, staff, and students to connect to and use campus computer networks.

While I'm content in my current role, it's always good to keep learning when working in the tech field. I also knew that I'd regret it forever if I didn't take advantage of the tuition benefits that come with working for a well-regarded university. To me, an MBA seemed like a natural choice: it's a widely-recognized credential across a variety of fields; it rounds out my beloved liberal arts education; and it's a terminal professional degree to boot. Come Spring 2026 (my graduation date), here's hoping that the MBA becomes the moniker of this blog: Matt's Big Achievement.

I'm not entirely sure what the next three years will bring, nor have I any clue what will come after the program. But the motivations behind this blog are threefold:

  1. To chronicle my journey towards earning an MBA while working full-time;
  2. To provide the perspective of a university staff member using his tuition benefits to earn a degree from said university (a perspective that was challenging to glean leading up to my enrollment in the program);
  3. To finally write a blog of some kind during a relatively significant life event, something that's been encouraged by family and friends (thanks, Mom).

My goal is to publish a post about once a week throughout the duration of the program. If you like what you see or have any questions, please reach out! I'm always looking to build out my network, and I'm happy to chat.
